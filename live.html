the holy sea

May 16: Live at Central Presbyterian Church • Austin, Texas

As an extension of the sound work, The Holy Sea is in production for performance as a live work of experimental music theatre. The live version features actors, movement artists, and musicians alongside multi-channel electronic sound. Featuring some of the most unique moments of the entire eight acts, the live version of The Holy Sea portrays scenes from each part and culminates into a singular, aggregated meta-Mass for sacred space.

Music by Mike Vernusky, directed by Khristián Khan and featuring theatrical work of Greg Romero, Scott Nyerges, and others. Collaborators include San Antonio-based breakdancer Tim Stuh, actors Kelly Hasandras, Megan Todd, and Caroline Beagles, with costume design by Jessi Lowerre and Stephanie Fisher.

Featured on this page are a few moments from the work.

Part One

Tim Stuh breakdances to a vinyl dance record from the 1980s, revealing the thunderous moment when God created excuses. despondency, laziness, procrastination, fear, slovenliness, and inconsiderateness. And it was so.

Part Two

Experience a wedding ceremony from 1963 in Allentown, Pennsylvania, where two people are joined in marriage. Here, beginning with the original organ playing the Wedding march, two women are married by Father Sound, speaking from inside of the speaker.

Part Three

A hypnotic regression unfolds in 1952, led by entrepreneur and amatuer hypnotist Morey Bernstein and featuring housewife Virginia Tighe, who claimed to be reincarnated from a 19th-century Irishwoman named Bridey Murphy in a past life.

Part Four

Greg Romero plays the Karatist Preacher, a man who would chop through blocks of concrete in the name of the Lord. Here he explains the different kinds of power you hear about these days, all of which pale in comparison to the Power of God.
